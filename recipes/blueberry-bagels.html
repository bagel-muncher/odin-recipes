Blueberry Bagels

Description

Blueberry bagels are my absolute favorite. I'm a big blueberry fan in general, so I'm probably biased when I say that blueberry bagels are the best type of bagel in the world. These bagels are always my "to-go" breakfast option.

Anyways, in light of Panera Bread's decision to discontinue their blueberry bagels, here is an amazing recipe to make amazing blueberry bagels. These bagels will come out as super chewy and rich with sweet blueberry flavor.

Note: This recipe is from Baking with Butter.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 cups freeze-dried blueberries
  • 1/2 cup + 1 tablespoon lukewarm water
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast or instant yeast
  • 2 Tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 3 1/2 cups bread flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Poaching
    • 2 liters water
    • 1 Tablespoon honey or brown sugar

Steps

  1. Step 1: Combine water, yeast, and sugar and stir in a large bowl or the bowl of your stand mixer fitted with a dough hook. If using active dry yeast, let this sit for around 5-10 minutes until it becomes foamy, if using instant yeast this step can be skipped.
  2. Step 2: Add in the flour, salt, blueberries, and vanilla. Use a fork or switch the stand mixer to low speed to combine it into a thick dough. It should be very thick and slightly sticky. If the dough is too dry, add a tablespoon or two of water as needed.
  3. Step 3: Keep it kneading or mixing for around 10 minutes for optimal gluten development.
  4. Step 4: Form the dough into a ball once it is kneaded and strong. Place the dough ball into a large oiled bowl and cover it with plastic wrap or a damp towel. Let the dough rise in a warm place until doubled in size.
  5. Step 5: Deflate the dough and pull it from the bowl onto a lightly floured surface.
  6. Step 6: Cut the dough into eight equal pieces (a kitchen scale works well to ensure even bagels) and shape each piece into a ball. Let these dough balls rest for 5 minutes.
  7. Step 7: Use both hands’ thumb and index finger to push a hole into the center of each ball of dough, then roll the dough ball around your fingers in a circular motion to widen the hole. Aim for a large hole, as it will shrink back in size once it sits.
  8. Step 8: Place the shaped bagels on a prepared sheet pan lined with parchment paper and let them puff out a bit for around 30 minutes while the oven preheats and a pot of water is brought to a boil.
  9. Step 9: Bring a large pot of water to a boil and preheat the oven to 425°F/220 °C. Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper.
  10. Step 10: Once the water is boiling, stir in honey or brown sugar.
  11. Step 11: Dust any excess flour off the bagels and drop them in the boiling water one at a time. Boil 2-3 bagels at a time.
  12. Step 12: Let them poach in the water for 1 minute total, flipping them after 30 seconds. Remove the bagels from the water using a slotted spoon. Drain the boiled bagels on a wire rack and continue with the rest of the bagels.
  13. Step 13: Place the bagels on the lined baking sheet. Bake them in the oven for 20-25 minutes or until deep golden brown. If they are baking unevenly or your oven has hot spots, turn the oven tray around after 15 minutes of baking.
  14. Step 14: Remove the baked bagels from the tray and let them cool on a cooling rack for 30 minutes before slicing and serving.

Enjoy!
